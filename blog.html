How Often Should You Really Detail Your Car in Idaho?

MBy Marten Mobile Detailing·June 24, 2026·6 min read
Listen instead

Prefer audio? Ava and Marcus break down the whole schedule in a quick two host episode.

Ask ten people how often they should detail their car and you will get ten answers. The truth is that here in the Treasure Valley, the right schedule has less to do with a calendar and more to do with how hard the road, the dust and the seasons are working against your paint and your interior. Edward at Marten Mobile Detailing sees the same patterns every week, so here is a straightforward guide to keeping your vehicle clean and protected without overthinking it.

Why Idaho is tough on vehicles

Nampa, Caldwell and the rest of the valley sit in high desert farm country, and that comes with a specific kind of wear. Summer brings long stretches of heat and strong sun that bake dust onto the paint and fade interiors that sit in a hot parking lot all day. Spring and fall kick up field dust and pollen that work their way into every vent and seam. Then winter rolls in with road grime, sand and the salty spray that gets thrown up on the interstate, all of which sticks to the lower panels and wheels and slowly eats at the finish if it is left there.

None of this is a reason to panic. It just means a car that gets washed twice a year is going to age faster than one that gets a little regular attention. The goal is not perfection. The goal is staying ahead of the buildup so your vehicle holds its value and stays a nice place to sit.

A simple schedule that actually works

For most people in the valley, a good rhythm looks like this. Give the exterior a proper wash every two weeks, do a real interior clean about once a month, book a full detail two to four times a year as the seasons change, and refresh any paint protection once a year or so. That is it. If you drive a lot of dirt roads, haul tools, or have kids and dogs in the back, you tighten that schedule up. If your car mostly lives in a garage, you can stretch it out.

The single most useful habit is the every two week exterior wash. That is the one that keeps grime, bugs and winter salt from sitting long enough to do damage. It is also the easiest thing to put off, which is exactly why so many of Edward customers set it on a recurring schedule and stop thinking about it.

The exterior

A real hand wash does a lot more than a quick drive-through pass. Wheels and tires get cleaned, bugs and road film get lifted off the paint, and the lower panels that catch the most grime actually get attention. A few times a year it is worth going further with a full decontamination that pulls out the bonded contaminants a normal wash leaves behind, which is what makes the paint feel smooth again instead of gritty.

If your vehicle has a ceramic coating, regular gentle washing is what keeps it working. And here is a small thing that says a lot about how Marten Mobile Detailing operates. One customer scheduled a full decontamination, and once Edward started he came back out to explain that the existing coating was still holding up well and they could skip that step. That kind of honest call is rare, and it is the reason people keep him on the calendar.

The interior

Interiors get neglected because the wear is gradual. Dust settles into the vents, crumbs work into the seats, and the carpets quietly collect everything your shoes bring in. A monthly interior clean keeps it from reaching the point where it needs a rescue. That means a full vacuum of carpets, seats, mats and trunk, a wipe down of the dash, console and door panels, and leather cleaned and conditioned so it does not dry out and crack in the summer heat.

When an interior has gone too long, a deep clean brings it right back. Customers regularly describe getting their vehicle back looking and smelling brand new, which is the standard Edward holds himself to on every job.

If you have pets

Dog owners are on their own schedule. Pet hair has a way of weaving into the carpet and seats where a normal vacuum cannot reach it, and odor builds up over time whether you notice it day to day or not. Targeted pet hair removal paired with an odor treatment makes a bigger difference than people expect, and it is one of the most requested services for exactly that reason. If you have a dog that rides along often, a regular interior reset is worth it.

Why mobile makes the schedule stick

The reason most detailing schedules fall apart is simple. Driving across town, dropping the car off and waiting around is a hassle, so it gets skipped. Marten Mobile Detailing removes that friction entirely by coming to you. Edward shows up at your home or your work, on time, with everything he needs. Customers point out again and again that when he says he will be there at nine, he is there at nine. When the clean comes to you, keeping a schedule stops being a chore.

DIY or call a pro?

There is nothing wrong with a quick rinse in the driveway between visits. A rinse keeps dust down and is fine for maintenance. The difference a professional makes shows up in the things that are easy to skip and easy to do wrong: safe wash technique that does not swirl the paint, real decontamination, proper leather care, and the kind of finish work that makes a vehicle look genuinely new instead of just less dirty. For the regular reset, and any time your car has gone a while, it pays to bring in someone who does this every day.

The short version

Wash the outside every couple of weeks, clean the inside monthly, book a full detail a few times a year, and keep any protection refreshed. Tighten it up if you drive hard or have pets, relax it if you garage the car.
